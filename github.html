Hamiltonian Snake

This odd snake game is not like the others. In this game you are not the player, rather an observer of this perfectionist snake as it continuously follows a specific generated circuit which is unique with each game. Behind the scenes, the program is generating a Hamiltonian Circuit which the snake will continuously follow until it fills out the board. An average full game takes around 6.5 minutes in order to get 400 apples and to turn into a 20x20 square.

Wired to Bluetooth Headphones

So, I conceived the idea for this project in April 2020, bored during quarantine. I was going through old tech of mine in hopes of finding something to occupy myself with and found that pair of headphones and a cheap pair of bluetooth headphones, and it struck me, turn those wired headphones into wireless. From there, I took apart the bluetooth headphones to see how it worked and found the board with the wireless transmitter with wires going to the speakers. Finally, I de-soldered the wires from the wireless headphones and soldered the wired headphone wires to the wireless transmitter board and it worked. It was a great opportunity and lesson in bluetooth and headphone hardware.

Bubble Fett Robot - Grade 11 Robotics

In grade 11 I joined the robotics team with three of my other friends and this is the robot we made. This was for the First Tech Competition by First Robotics and we worked on it passively over several months, struggling to find time to be able to work on it because of Covid. We competed in a couple competitions with it and performed alright for it being our first time doing robotics. I took the role of lead programmer and worked in Android Studio with Java to interface through an Android app to the onboard control hub and worked with Autonomous and TeleOp modes of control. Ultimately, it was a great experience which I hope to be able to continue with.
